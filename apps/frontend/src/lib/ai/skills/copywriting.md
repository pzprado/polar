# Copywriting Skill

Write clear, friendly UI text that helps users understand what's happening and what to do next. The audience is creators building apps — they're not technical, so keep language simple and encouraging.

## Labels & Buttons

- Use specific verb + object: "Send Tip", "Join Game", "Mint Token" — not "Submit" or "OK".
- For destructive actions: "Delete" not "Remove", and show what's being deleted.
- Primary CTA should clearly state the outcome: "Publish App", "Create Rewards Program".
- Keep button text to 2-4 words.

## Error Messages

Every error should answer three questions:
1. What happened? (brief, specific)
2. Why? (if helpful)
3. How to fix it? (actionable next step)

Examples:
- Bad: "Transaction failed"
- Good: "Transaction failed — your wallet doesn't have enough AVAX. You need at least 0.1 AVAX to join."
- Bad: "Error"
- Good: "Couldn't connect to your wallet. Make sure MetaMask is unlocked and try again."

Never use humor in error messages. Be direct and helpful.

## Empty States

Empty states should teach, not just say "nothing here":
- Explain what this area will show once there's data.
- Include a clear action to get started.
- Keep it warm and encouraging.

Examples:
- Bad: "No transactions"
- Good: "No tips yet. Share your page link to start receiving tips."
- Bad: "No players"
- Good: "Waiting for players to join. The game starts when 2 players are in."

## Loading States

Be specific about what's loading:
- "Connecting to your wallet..." not "Loading..."
- "Sending your tip..." not "Processing..."
- "Checking game results..." not "Please wait..."

## Tone

- Confident, not hedging: "Your app is live" not "Your app should be live now"
- Friendly, not corporate: "Nice — your token is ready" not "Token creation successful"
- Brief, not verbose: every word must earn its place
- Honest, not sensational: don't overclaim or use exclamation marks
